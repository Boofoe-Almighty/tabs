==== Notes ====

This is one of the simpler and more straightforward "doom" tracks on Dead Roots Stirring, with
no shortage of heavy, '70s-inflected Sabbath riffs. It doesn't take much digging to reveal the
additional melodic/harmonic complexity, however. It's one of the quintessential DRS-era Elder songs
in that regard. You'd be forgiven for thinking of this doom metal and, at the same time, something
far more interesting.

Tonally, like the rest of DRS, it seems very simple. I'm guessing...

SG > CAE Wah > BAT Pharaoh > (some kind of reverb/delay) > Sound City 120 (DR103 circuit)

And maybe a phaser in a few parts as well?

Guitar I is, generally speaking, panned left, with Guitar II on the right (except in some cases
wherein both tracks are mixed to the center, which I've noted). Sometimes one of the guitar tracks
is darker/fuzzier while the other is cleaner/crunchier, and other times they're both pretty heavily
fuzzed-out.

Video footage used for corroboration/reference...

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vzjd5y2PDM0
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VNtT46PdSWY
